Use quadratic formula: \(x = \frac{8 \pm \sqrt{(-8)^2 - 4(2)(6)}}{2(2)}\)
Simplify: \(x = \frac{8 \pm \sqrt{64 - 48}}{4} = \frac{8 \pm \sqrt{16}}{4}\)

12. Solve: \(x^2 + x - 12 = 0\)
Factor: \((x + 4)(x - 3) = 0\)
Solutions: \(x = -4\) or \(x = 3\)
13. Solve: \(4x^2 - 9 = 0\)
Difference of squares: \((2x - 3)(2x + 3) = 0\)
Solutions: \(x = \frac{3}{2}\) or \(x = -\frac{3}{2}\)
14. Solve: \(x^2 + 2x + 1 = 0\)
Perfect square: \((x + 1)^2 = 0\)
Solution: \(x = -1\)
15. Solve: \(3x^2 + 10x + 7 = 0\)
Use quadratic formula: \(x = \frac{-10 \pm \sqrt{(10)^2 - 4(3)(7)}}{2(3)}\)
Simplify: \(x = \frac{-10 \pm \sqrt{100 - 84}}{6} = \frac{-10 \pm \sqrt{16}}{6}\)
Final: \(x = \frac{-10 \pm 4}{6} \Rightarrow x = -1\) or \(x = -\frac{7}{3}\)
16. Solve: \(x^2 - 3x - 10 = 0\)
Factor: \((x - 5)(x + 2) = 0\)
Solutions: \(x = 5\) or \(x = -2\)
17. Solve: \(2x^2 + 5x - 3 = 0\)
Use quadratic formula: \(x = \frac{-5 \pm \sqrt{(5)^2 - 4(2)(-3)}}{2(2)}\)
Simplify: \(x = \frac{-5 \pm \sqrt{25 + 24}}{4} = \frac{-5 \pm \sqrt{49}}{4}\)
Final: \(x = \frac{-5 + 7}{4} = \frac{1}{2},\ x = \frac{-5 - 7}{4} = -3\)
18. Solve: \(x^2 - 10x + 25 = 0\)
Perfect square: \((x - 5)^2 = 0\)
Solution: \(x = 5\)
19. Solve: \(x(x - 7) = 60\)
Expand: \(x^2 - 7x = 60 \Rightarrow x^2 - 7x - 60 = 0\)
Factor: \((x - 12)(x + 5) = 0\)
Solutions: \(x = 12\) or \(x = -5\)
20. Solve: \(2x^2 - 3x = 2\)
Bring all terms to one side: \(2x^2 - 3x - 2 = 0\)
Use quadratic formula: \(x = \frac{3 \pm \sqrt{(-3)^2 - 4(2)(-2)}}{2(2)}\)
Simplify: \(x = \frac{3 \pm \sqrt{9 + 16}}{4} = \frac{3 \pm \sqrt{25}}{4}\)
Final: \(x = \frac{3 + 5}{4} = 2,\ x = \frac{3 - 5}{4} = -0.5\)
21. Solve: \( x^2 + 1 = 0 \) Step 1: Move the constant → \( x^2 = -1 \) Step 2: Take square root → \( x = \pm \sqrt{-1} \) Final Answer: No real solution (imaginary).
22. Solve: \( 3x^2 + 7x + 2 = 0 \) Use quadratic formula: \( x = \frac{-7 \pm \sqrt{7^2 - 4(3)(2)}}{2(3)} = \frac{-7 \pm \sqrt{49 - 24}}{6} = \frac{-7 \pm \sqrt{25}}{6} \) \( x = \frac{-7 \pm 5}{6} \Rightarrow x = -\frac{1}{3} \) or \( x = -2 \)
23. Solve: \( x(x + 2) = 24 \) Step 1: Expand → \( x^2 + 2x = 24 \) Step 2: Move terms → \( x^2 + 2x - 24 = 0 \) Step 3: Factor → \( (x + 6)(x - 4) = 0 \) Final Answer: \( x = -6 \) or \( x = 4 \)
24. Solve: \( x^2 - 6x + 9 = 0 \) Step 1: Recognize perfect square → \( (x - 3)^2 = 0 \) Final Answer: \( x = 3 \)
25. Solve: \( x^2 + 11x + 28 = 0 \) Step 1: Factor → \( (x + 4)(x + 7) = 0 \) Final Answer: \( x = -4 \) or \( x = -7 \)
26. Solve: \( x^2 - 8x = 20 \) Step 1: Move terms → \( x^2 - 8x - 20 = 0 \) Step 2: Use quadratic formula: \( x = \frac{8 \pm \sqrt{(-8)^2 + 80}}{2} = \frac{8 \pm \sqrt{144}}{2} = \frac{8 \pm 12}{2} \) Final Answer: \( x = -2 \) or \( x = 10 \)
27. Solve: \( x^2 = x + 6 \) Step 1: Move terms → \( x^2 - x - 6 = 0 \) Step 2: Factor → \( (x - 3)(x + 2) = 0 \) Final Answer: \( x = 3 \) or \( x = -2 \)
28. Solve: \( x(x - 5) = -14 \) Step 1: Expand → \( x^2 - 5x = -14 \) Step 2: Move terms → \( x^2 - 5x + 14 = 0 \) Step 3: Use quadratic formula: \( x = \frac{5 \pm \sqrt{25 - 56}}{2} = \frac{5 \pm \sqrt{-31}}{2} \) Final Answer: No real solution (complex).
29. Solve: \( 2x^2 + 3x - 2 = 0 \) Step 1: Use quadratic formula: \( x = \frac{-3 \pm \sqrt{9 + 16}}{4} = \frac{-3 \pm \sqrt{25}}{4} = \frac{-3 \pm 5}{4} \) Final Answer: \( x = \frac{1}{2} \) or \( x = -2 \)
30. Solve: \( x^2 + 3x = 10 \) Step 1: Move terms → \( x^2 + 3x - 10 = 0 \) Step 2: Factor → \( (x + 5)(x - 2) = 0 \) Final Answer: \( x = -5 \) or \( x = 2 \)

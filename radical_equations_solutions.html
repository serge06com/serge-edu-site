Grade 12 Algebra – Radical Equations (Solutions)

  1. Solve for x: √(x + 5) = 3
    ➤ Square both sides: x + 5 = 9
    ➤ Subtract 5: x = 4
  2. Solve: √(2x - 3) + 4 = 7
    ➤ Subtract 4 from both sides: √(2x - 3) = 3
    ➤ Square both sides: 2x - 3 = 9
    ➤ Add 3: 2x = 12
    ➤ Divide by 2: x = 6
  3. Solve: √(x - 1) - √(x - 6) = 1
    ➤ Step 1: Isolate one radical: √(x - 1) = 1 + √(x - 6)
    ➤ Step 2: Square both sides:
    Left: (√(x - 1))² = x - 1
    Right: (1 + √(x - 6))² = 1 + 2√(x - 6) + (x - 6) = x - 5 + 2√(x - 6)
    ➤ So: x - 1 = x - 5 + 2√(x - 6)
    ➤ Subtract x - 5 from both sides: 4 = 2√(x - 6)
    ➤ Divide by 2: 2 = √(x - 6)
    ➤ Square both sides: x - 6 = 4
    ➤ x = 10
